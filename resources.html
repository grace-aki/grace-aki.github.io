Ways to Get Involved

The best way to learn about kalo is to get your hands in the mud. Below you'll find organizations with volunteer opportunities to work in loʻi across Oʻahu, tips for beginners, educational resources, and links to learn more. Many offer regular volunteer days. Please check their websites or contact them directly for up-to-date information.

Ka Papa Loʻi o Kānewai

2645 Dole St.
Honolulu, HI 96822

Located at UH Mānoa with community workdays on the first Saturday of every month.

Ka Papa Loʻi ʻo Punaluʻu

53-270 Kamehameha Highway
Hauʻula, HI 96717

Nestled on the windward side, community service days are on the third Saturday of every month.

Kākoʻo ʻŌiwi

46-406 Kamehameha Highway
Kāneʻohe, HI 96744

Come care for Heʻeia's wetland fields throughout the week on Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, and Friday.

Tips for First-Time Volunteers

  • Check volunteer information and schedules ahead of time
  • Wear clothes and shoes you don't mind getting muddy; bring a change of clothes for after
  • Bring sun/bug protection (reef-safe sunscreen, hat, bug spray), plenty of water, and a towel
  • Come with a ready-to-work attitude, an open heart, and an open mind to learning
  • Optional: gloves, reusable bag, light jacket

More Volunteer Opportunities

  • Kaʻala Farm – Living classroom in Waiʻanae. Community workday on the second Saturday of the month.
  • Hoʻoulu ʻĀina – 100-acre nature preserve in Kalihi Valley. Lend a hand here every other Saturday.
  • Kōkua Learning Farm – Haleiwa family-friendly learning farm. Get involved every first Saturday.
  • MAʻO Organic Farms – Hawaiʻi's largest certified organic farm located in Waiʻanae. Service days are on the last Saturday of the month. Connect with them on Instagram.

Learn More

  • Loʻi Virtual Field Trip – Kamehameha Schools offers a virtual tour of Ka Papa Loʻi ʻo Punaluʻu with additional educational resources.
  • College of Tropical Agriculture and Human Resilience (CTAHR) – Publications about kalo from UH Mānoa's agriculture college program.
  • Bishop Museum – Overview of kalo from a Native Hawaiian history museum.
  • Office of Hawaiian Affairs – Wealth of resources beneficial to the Native Hawaiian community.
  • Native Planters in Old Hawaii: Their Life, Lore, and Environment – Book written by E. S. Craighill Handy, Elizabeth Green Handy, and Mary Kawena Pukui about Hawaiian horticulture practices and their practitioners.
